Hello, I am Vidushi Sharma

Currently pursuing a Ph.D. degree focussed on computational study of heterostructure electrodes for Lithium ion batteries, in New Jersey Institute of Technology, US.

I came to United States in August 2017 to join Ph.D. programme at Department of Mechanical Engineering, New Jersey Institute of Technology(NJIT) under the guidance of Dr. Dibakar Datta. My Advisor Dr. Datta gave me the freedom to learn and explore the realm of computational simulations. Since then, I have worked on multiple projects focussed on two dimensional nanomaterials and garnered a vast simulation skill set and experience.

I was facinated with future promise of nanotechnology during my undergrad years(2011-2015) in India. That led me to become part of numerous researh groups as an Intern and gain experimental experience on synthesis and biological applications of nanoparticles. I have studied body distribution and deposition of Gold nanoparticles in Zebrafishes. This work was a turning point in my research goal. It was a great learning experience which came at the cost of 'living' (bless the zebrafishes that died during the study). Post this, I decided to dedicate myself to computational studies of nanomaterials and assist experimentalist. I did my Masters in Molecular Engineering and Advanced Chemical Analysis at National Institute of Technology, Kurukshetra, India after qualifying a national level Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering(GATE). I am passionate about technologies that hold potential to bring about a change in the lives of people for good.

In recent years I dedicate my time to work on materials with applications in clean energy for better future, learn new set of computational skills and create short learning materials for students in developing countries. I am fond of art and culture. I like to explore places of cultural significance. I spend my most spare time reading a book and I aspire to be a writer some day.

“Knowledge is Power.” -Motto I live with

My Skills

I am experienced in molecular and atomistic simulations. This includes Density Functional Theory(DFT), Ab-initio Molecular Dynamics(AIMD), Molecular Dynamics(MD), Kinetic Monte Carlo (KMC) and Molecular Docking. Softwares I am proficient in are VASP, LAMMPS, Gaussian, ChemDraw, and ones I am familiar with include Spartan and AutoDock Vina. I can say I am 92% efficient in these skills.

I also started using Artificial Intelligence for material simulations in recent years. I can develop Machine Learning and Deep Learning algorithms in Tensorflow package of Python for supervised learning and classification of materials. This is sphere where I am growing as a researcher and so, I am not very efficient yet.

With little observation and minimal training, I can succesfully carry out organic, inorganic and organometallic synthesis in laboratory settings. This holds true for characterization as well. X-ray powder diffraction(XRD), Scanning electron microscope(SEM), Nuclear magnetic resonance(NMR), Column chromatography, Infrared Spectroscopy(IR) and Spectrophotometric assays are a few I have commonly performed.
